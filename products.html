01
India.
Discription: Indian coal is primarily used for power generation. It has high ash content and moderate energy value, making it more suitable for domestic consumption or blending.
Best uses: Thermal power plants, cement industries.
Coal Type: Mostly thermal coal (bituminous & sub-bituminous), and some lignite.
Qualities:
  • High ash content (35-45%)
  • Low sulfur
  • Moderate to low calorific value
Features:
  • Mainly used for power generation
  • Requires blending or beneficiation to meet industrial standards
02
Indonesian.
Discription: Known for its low ash and low sulfur content, Indonesian coal is lightweight, eco-friendly, and cost-effective. It's ideal for boilers and thermal plants.
Best uses: Blending, industrial boilers, thermal power.
Coal Type: Sub-bituminous and low-rank bituminous
Qualities:
  • Low ash (3-10%)
  • Low sulfur "(<1%)"
  • Medium moisture (10-20%)
  • Gross Calorific Value: 4,000 to 6,000 kcal/kg
Features:
  • Ideal for power plants (thermal use)
  • Popular for blending with higher ash coals (like Indian)
  • Eco-friendly and cheaper shipping costs due to proximity in Asia
03
United Stats of America.
Discription: American coal offers high energy and consistent quality. With low impurities and good carbon content, it supports both industrial and steelmaking needs.
Best uses: Metallurgical and thermal power sectors.
Coal Type: Bituminous, anthracite, and sub-bituminous
Qualities:
  • High energy content (up to 7,000 kcal/kg)
  • Low to moderate ash
  • High fixed carbon
  • Low sulfur in some grades (especially Appalachian coal)
Features:
  • Excellent for metallurgical and thermal purposes
  • Reliable quality with advanced mining tech
04
Australian.
Discription: Globally renowned for its premium quality, especially in hard coking coal. It delivers high heat, low impurities, and strong coke for steel production.
Best uses: Steel manufacturing, metallurgical industries.
Coal Type: Premium coking coal, bituminous (thermal & metallurgical)
Qualities:
  • Very high calorific value (6,000–7,200 kcal/kg)
  • Very low moisture & ash
  • Low sulfur
Features:
  • World's best hard coking coal for steelmaking
  • Top exporter globally; stable quality
  • Preferred for high-end industrial applications
05
Russian.
Discription: Russian coal is high in carbon and energy content. It is dependable for power generation and industrial use, with competitive export pricing.
Best uses: Power plants, industrial boilers, blending.
Coal Type: Bituminous and lignite
Qualities:
  • High fixed carbon
  • Low sulfur (in some deposits)
  • Calorific value between 5,500–6,800 kcal/kg
Features:
  • Competitive pricing
  • Good blending coal
  • Suitable for both power and industrial use
06
South Affrican.
Discription: South African coal is known for its balanced price-performance ratio. It’s widely exported for its reliability in large-scale thermal applications.
Best uses: Power plants, export blending, cement plants.
Coal Type: Bituminous
Qualities:
  • High ash (15–25%)
  • Medium sulfur
  • Calorific value: 5,000–6,000 kcal/kg
Features:
  • Widely used in power stations (thermal)
  • Popular for exports to India & Asia
  • Competitive pricing
07
Magnese Ore.
Discription: Manganese ore is a key component in steel production and alloy manufacturing. High-grade ores have high Mn content and minimal impurities.
Best uses: Steel alloying, battery industry, chemical manufacturing.
Coal Type: Varies by Mn content (typically 30%–50% Mn)
Qualities:
  • High Mn = High steelmaking value
  • Low impurities like Fe, Al, Si preferred
Features:
  • Key alloying agent in steel manufacturing
  • Also used in batteries, ceramics, and chemicals
  • High-quality ores from South Africa, Gabon, Australia
08
Cooking Coal.
Discription: Coking coal is a vital raw material in steelmaking. It forms strong, porous coke under high heat, which is essential in blast furnace operations.
Best uses: Iron and steel industries (blast furnaces).
Coal Type: Hard coking coal, Semi-soft coking coal, PCI coal
Qualities:
  • High fixed carbon (>60%)
  • Low volatile matter (VM)
  • Low ash "(<10%)" & sulfur (<1%)
Features:
  • Essential in steelmaking (forms coke in blast furnace)
  • High swelling index and coke strength
  • Mostly sourced from Australia, USA, and Canada
